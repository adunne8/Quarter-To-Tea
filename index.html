Quarter To Tea

A modern and quirky tea shop in the heart of the city centre. Come sample some of our delicious brews and share a pot with friends. Or purchase some of our speciality teas and the equipment to make your own tea parties in the comfort of your own home!

Our Services

Tea Service

Enjoy some of the finest blends of teas all acrooss the world, expertly prepaired by our master brewer

Tea Sampling

Not sure what to try, maybe a bit overwhelmed by choice? Our staff will provide excellent suggestions based on their own experience. All our staff are tea fanatics and can help you find the right brew for you along with samples to try

Brew Your Own

Find a tea you like? Why not purchase a bag of frshly imported leaves and prepare a tea party for your friends. Havent got the equipment? We can sort you out with all manner of brewing equipment, from a basic teapot to speciality stirrers, we can help anyone from the first explorer to the most fanatic of tea drinkers!

Who Are We

We started out as a group of college students, studting collinary science in DIT together. Inbetween intense study sessions and project deadlines we ofthen found ourselves together looking for somewhere to relax and chat together. We started arguing the age old argument of Barry or Lyons, which quickly evolved into the more inticate discussions as to the merrits of each type of tea. Unfortunately we found the Dublin area lacking for places to source and sample some of the delicious brews de discovered while on various trips abroad. We began to dream about a place where we could share our discoveries together. We soon realised we were the perfoect group to setup our own place. Passionate, knowledgeable and just innocent enough to think setting up a company would be easy. Well it wasn't easy but we've made it this far. Why not pop in and see what we have in stock this week

Why 'Quarter To Tea'?

its a bit of an inside joke between us. We always had 15 mins between classes to catch up together and an old saying, "What time is it? A Quarter to Tea" quickly emerged.

...We still regularly argue who originally said this phrase
